496 SOWING WE DO

Sowing we do at the breaking of dawn,

Sowing we do when the sun has risen;

Sowing in daytime, also till night,

Sowing at nighttime, till morning breaks.

CHORUS

Oh! //What harvest will I reap?//

When in the light or in darkness I sowed;

Anything sown will be gathered in time,

And in that time, a fruit it will give;

Oh this will be in the eternity.

2

Sowing we do in barren pasture lands,

Sowing through bones of the creatures who died;

Sowing with the wind blowing around,

Sowing the fertile land that I have found.

3

We sow tenaciously with many cries,

Sowing the squeezed honey into the soul;

Sowings of faith creates a peace,

Harvest and crowns to the faithful are joy.
