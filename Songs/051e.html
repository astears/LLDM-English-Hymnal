051 MANSIONS OVER THE HILLTOP

I'm satisfied with just a cottage below,

A little silver a little gold;

But in that city where the ransomed will shine,

I want a gold one that's silver lined.

CHORUS

I've got a mansion just over the hilltop,

In that bright land where Christ awaits me;

And some day yonder we will never more wander,

But walk on streets that are purest gold.

2

Tho' often tempted, tormented, and tested,

And like a prophet my pillow stone;

And though I find here, no permanent dwelling,

I know He'll give me, home of my own.

3

Don't think me poor, or deserted, or lonely,

I'm not discouraged I'm heaven bound;

I'm just a pilgrim in search of a city,

I want a mansion, a robe, and a crown.

4

The streets of gold and the doors lined with pearls,

A wall of jasper, no temple there;

No moon or sunshine, it will be so pretty,

The peace of Jesus will be its light.
