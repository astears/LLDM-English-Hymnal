024 UPON MOUNT OF OLIVES

Upon Mount of Olives, there was a great pleading,

A man who was prostrate in a humble prayer;

In it He was seeking the divine protection,

The sorrow was bitter that fled from His heart.

CHORUS

In solitude, in that painful and such bitter anguish,

He endured for His love;

Only there, with the songbirds He had formed a concert,

Lifting up His cries.

2

This way He was taken, my Jesus to Calvary,

And with Him the sins of all humanity;

His death was then sentenced, in exchange for his life,

And for their benefit , He drank vinegar.

3

And when Jesus passed on at the cross in Calvary,

He claimed to the heavens, lifting up his voice;

This way He surrendered His glorious spirit,

With it consummating the covenant of God.

4

And when my soul wandered lost in all the darkness,

I heard of the message of a Man of God;

And it was through Jesus the hope of salvation,

In which an Apostle was sent from above.
