199 ONE HUNDRED WERE COUNTED

One hundred were counted,

lambs of the Shepherd's flock,

One hundred were counted,

He took them with love;

But then one sad evening,

He began to count them,

//Surely one was missing// and sadly He cried.

CHORUS

Ninety-nine He left there, in the flock behind Him,

Through the mountains searching, the Shepherd went on;

Then He found her weeping, trembling cold and lonely,

//Her wounds he anointed// and took her back home.

2

So this same old story, is being repeated,

There are still sheep missing, wandering all alone;

Wandering in this world, nowhere near the flock,

//Without God or love// without forgiveness.
