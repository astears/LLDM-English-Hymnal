454 WHO IS HE THAT COMES FROM EDOM?

Who is he that comes from Edom?

His attire is all red; I can see His hands are punctured,

And His side is cruelly hurt.

Who's this man of many burdens?

The one that is badly hurt;

Tell me why have they disfigured,

His beautiful noble face?

CHORUS

It is Jesus, the King of glory,

With His death He ransomed me;

Come to worship and proclaim Him,

He's our Prince He is our Lord.

2

Who is He that's bearing disdain,

On the winepress walked alone?

By all men He was forgotten,

No consolation for Him.

When in anguish and affliction,

No complaint was heard from Him;

Like a lamb to death departed,

He was mute up till His death.

3

He is wearing a King's garment,

And a crown He bears it's true;

But it's not golden its of thorns,

And His scepter's but a stick.

Why do they mock Him so coldly?

And they whip Him without fear;

In His kind face they are spitting,

Perhaps He was a convict?

4

Who is this man in the summit,

Of the Calvary He died?

He prayed for His enemies there,

He forgives their wickedness.

Still the earth shakes and it trembles,

And its clouds cover the sun;

As His head inclines before them,

And to God He gives His soul.
