112 WHEN MY LIFE WORK HAS ENDED

When my life work has ended and I cross the swelling tide,

When the bright and glorious morning I will see;

I will call my Redeemer when I reach the other side,

And His smile will be the first to welcome me.

CHORUS

I will know Him, I will know Him,

All the redeemed by his side I shall surely stand;

I will know Him, I will know Him,

By the print of the nails in His hands.

2

Oh, the soul thrilling rapture when I view His blessed face,

And the luster of His kindly beaming eye;

How my heart full of joy will praise His love, grace, and mercy,

That prepare for me a mansion in the sky.

3

Oh, the dear ones in glory, how they'll beckon me to come,

And our parting at the river I recall;

In the valleys of Eden they'll be singing: welcome home,

But I long to meet my Savior first of all.

4

Through the gates to the city in a robe of spotless white,

He will lead me where no tears will ever fall;

In the glad song of ages I will mingle with delight,

But I long to meet my Savior first of all.
