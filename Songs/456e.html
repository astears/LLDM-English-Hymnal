456 WHO WILL BE MY BONDSMAN?

Who will be my bondsman? I asked after I left,

Forever the pathways corrupted by sin;

When the almighty judge before me I did see,

Who will I have in the end: as a justice for me?

CHORUS

When the almighty judge comes to my soul to weigh,

And a bondsman He asks me for my immortal soul;

The Lord Jesus Christ will come and defend me,

And exclaim with great love: I will respond for thee.

2

Oh how dark was my past, that I saw on the cross,

Oh how many vile sins in the pathway I walked;

For there were no good things to be found in my life,

Who will then for my soul, want to respond for me?

3

It was justice whom I saw when brought to the scale,

And I trembled and moaned as I stepped on the scale;

As I weighed myself there, and I saw all my faults,

Who will then for my soul, want to respond for me?
